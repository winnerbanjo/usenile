Why Snapchat Ads Are the Most Underrated Marketing Channel for Nigerian Businesses

By Winner Oyebanjo • July 2026

If you're spending all your advertising budget on Instagram and Facebook, you're probably making the same mistake thousands of Nigerian businesses are making every day.

While everyone is competing for attention on Meta, there's another platform quietly delivering incredible results for brands that know how to use it.

That platform is Snapchat.

For many business owners, Snapchat is still seen as an app for sending disappearing pictures. In reality, it's one of the most powerful advertising platforms available today, especially if your audience includes Gen Z and Millennials.

If you're looking for cheaper advertising costs, less competition, and a new source of customers, it might be time to give Snapchat Ads a serious look.

Why Most Businesses Ignore Snapchat

When people think about digital advertising, they usually think about:

  • Facebook Ads
  • Instagram Ads
  • TikTok Ads
  • Google Ads

Snapchat rarely makes the list.

That's exactly why it's such an opportunity.

Less competition often means your ads can reach more people for the same budget compared to more crowded platforms.

Snapchat's Audience Is More Valuable Than You Think

Snapchat has hundreds of millions of active users worldwide, and many of them open the app dozens of times each day. The platform is especially strong among younger audiences who are highly engaged with short-form, vertical content.

If your business sells products or services to people between 18 and 35 years old, Snapchat deserves to be part of your marketing strategy.

Industries that can perform particularly well include:

  • Fashion
  • Beauty
  • Skincare
  • Hair
  • Food
  • Fitness
  • Events
  • Gadgets
  • Online stores
  • Mobile apps

Why Snapchat Ads Can Be Cheaper

One reason many marketers are getting good results is simple. There are fewer advertisers competing for the same audience compared to Facebook and Instagram.

That often translates into:

  • Lower advertising costs
  • Better visibility
  • Higher engagement
  • Less ad fatigue

Of course, your results still depend on your targeting, creative, and offer, but many advertisers report strong performance when targeting younger audiences.

Snapchat Is Built for Mobile

Unlike many advertising platforms that adapted to mobile, Snapchat was designed around it from day one. Everything happens in vertical, full-screen experiences.

Your ads don't compete with dozens of distractions. Instead, users experience immersive content that fills the screen.

Snap itself recommends short, authentic vertical videos with your main message shown within the first few seconds.

@thewinnerbanjo

The Biggest Mistake Businesses Make

Many businesses simply copy their Instagram ads and upload them to Snapchat.

That usually doesn't work.

Snapchat users expect content that feels natural and native. Instead of polished commercials, create videos that look like someone picked up their phone and started talking.

Simple content often performs better than highly produced advertisements.

What Makes a Great Snapchat Ad?

The best-performing Snapchat ads usually have:

  • A strong hook in the first two seconds
  • Vertical video format
  • Clear, readable captions
  • Fast pacing
  • A simple call to action
  • Real people instead of stock footage

Snapchat also offers formats such as Single Image or Video Ads, Story Ads, Collection Ads, and Sponsored Snaps, allowing businesses to choose the format that matches their campaign goals.

Is Snapchat Right for Every Business?

Not necessarily. If your audience is primarily older professionals or you're targeting a niche B2B market, LinkedIn or Google Ads may be a better fit.

However, if your customers are active on social media and spend time consuming short-form video content, Snapchat is worth testing.

Never assume a platform won't work until you've actually tested it.

My Advice

One of the biggest advantages in digital marketing comes from going where everyone else isn't.

While thousands of businesses are fighting for attention on Meta, smart marketers are quietly testing channels that receive less competition.

You don't need to move your entire budget overnight. Start with a small test budget. Measure your results. Compare your cost per customer against your Facebook, Instagram, or TikTok campaigns.

You might be surprised by what you discover.

Final Thoughts

The best marketing platform isn't always the most popular one. It's the platform that delivers the best return on your investment.

Snapchat Ads remain one of the most overlooked opportunities for businesses that want to reach younger audiences with engaging, mobile-first advertising.

If you've never considered Snapchat before, now might be the perfect time to test it.

Your competitors probably aren't there yet.
